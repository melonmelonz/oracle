# PEEK and MUTE

## PEEK, the capstone

PEEK is Penn's capstone project — a recursive-acronym name in the tradition of
GNU, where the word stands for itself all the way down. It is offline-first and
built with a prison context in mind, a constraint that shapes every
choice toward working without a network. The first module of PEEK is an
eldritch-tamagotchi: a neon TUI creature you keep alive in the terminal, equal
parts pet and omen.

## MUTE, the game

MUTE is "a bit that can't speak" — an Unreal Engine 5 meta-game. The premise is a
single bit, a creature without a voice, navigating a world that keeps changing
the rules on it. The structure is five sessions, each its own dimension, threaded
with programming puzzles, and the whole thing is pitched in a Cowboy-Bebop tone:
melancholy, cool, a little jazzy, never self-serious.

Development happens in a dedicated VM at ~/dev/peek-vm, the spec lives under the
superpowers specs directory, and the repository is melonmelonz/mute.

## The shared thread

Both projects share a sensibility: small voiceless protagonists, terminals and
constraints treated as aesthetic rather than limitation, and a refusal to make
the interface explain itself. They are about what a tiny thing can do when the
world is bigger than it and won't slow down.
