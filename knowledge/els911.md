# ELS911 and School Safety Mapping

## The work

ELS911 is school-safety infrastructure work — wayfinding, mapping, and emergency
floor plans for large school campuses. There are two distinct things that share
the name and should not be confused: the marketing site (els911.com) and a
separate portal product.

## Floor-plan scanning

The scanning rig is an FJD Trion P1 — a SLAM scanner with no GNSS — paired with
the Trion Model v2.05 desktop software. The pipeline turns raw scans of big
schools into 2D floor plans exported as DXF.

A few hard-won software notes:

- **Floor Extract is a filter, not a planning tool** — it isolates a slice, it
  doesn't author the plan.
- Hidden cloud returns are recovered through Switch Display rather than lost.
- Georeferencing goes through Coordinate Transformation, but for indoor school
  plans it is usually unneeded.

## The map PDF rendering

The deliverable map PDFs follow NFPA conventions. The legend is a vertical list
with one row per marker type, and hallway markers collapse into chips. Markers
render exactly where they were placed — no automatic separation, no leader lines
— and icon sizes are kept small so natural clustering reads correctly instead of
being spread artificially.

## The marketing site

els911.com is built at github.com/melonmelonz/els911site with a working tree at
~/dev/els911site. Unlike most of the other Pages projects, it deploys by direct
wrangler push rather than through git. It must not be confused with the separate
els911-portal codebase.
