# About Penn

## Who he is

Penn is a systems-track graduate-school applicant — a kernel and Rust learner who
thinks and talks at the level of design tradeoffs rather than recipes. His work
spans low-level systems (Rust-for-Linux kernel drivers, embedded bring-up) and a
sprawl of distinctive web projects, most of them deployed to Cloudflare Pages.

## How he works

He edits in vim, not VS Code — references to editing are in vim's idiom, commands
and plugins included. He prefers fewer clarifying questions: infer from context
and ship, and only stop to ask when an ambiguity is genuinely load-bearing. On
projects where he has handed over judgment — Wanderlost is the clearest example —
he wants continuous additive polish and trusts the design calls to be made
without check-ins.

## His prose voice

Penn writes with a varied cadence and concrete specifics, no em dashes, and lets
hedges and gentle self-deprecation land naturally. A writing sample lives with
his applications. His resume is both a local PDF and a live site at
pcporterfield-cv.pages.dev/resume.

## Hardware taste

He has opinions about gear. The default recommendation leans toward non-Pi
single-board computers — Radxa, BeagleBoard, Pine64 — and quality over price
within a budget, on the principle that the hardware should outlast the project he
bought it for. For logic analyzers he is a lifelong Saleae loyalist: the Saleae
Logic is the default rec, and alternatives only come up if he asks for them.

## The shape of the portfolio

Across everything — Goolz, Wanderlost, PEEK, MUTE, the kernel quest — there is a
consistent sensibility: retro and eldritch textures, small voiceless or cursed
protagonists, terminals and constraints treated as aesthetic, and a refusal to
let interfaces over-explain themselves. The systems work and the art are the same
taste pointed at different layers of the stack.
