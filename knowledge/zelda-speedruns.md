# Zelda Speedrunning

## The crown jewel: Ocarina of Time

The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time is one of the most studied speed games in
existence. Two decades of runners have taken it apart frame by frame, and the
result is a category list that ranges from "beat the whole game intended" to "trick
the console into rolling the credits in a few minutes." The any% record has fallen
from around half an hour, in the early intended-route days, to a handful of minutes
once arbitrary code execution entered the picture. The category names are their own
language: any%, 100%, MST (Medallions, Stones, and Trials), Glitchless, and All
Dungeons, among others.

## How the routes broke open

The collapse of the any% time happened in jumps, each one a new class of glitch.

- **Wrong Warp.** Loading a scene transition while the game is confused about which
  area you are entering sends Link to the wrong place, including, eventually, the
  ending. This is what first took any% from dungeon-by-dungeon down to a short run.
- **Reverse Bottle Adventure (RBA).** By manipulating which item the game thinks is
  in a bottle, runners could write unintended values into memory, unlocking items
  and events out of order.
- **Stale Reference Manipulation (SRM).** A deeper trick where the game keeps using
  a pointer to an object that no longer exists, letting a runner steer what that
  stale reference reads or writes. SRM made previously impossible setups routine.
- **Arbitrary Code Execution (ACE).** The endgame, literally. By arranging specific
  values in memory, often through the inventory and a bottle, runners can make the
  game execute data as code and jump straight to the credits. This is why the
  fastest any% looks nothing like playing the game.

## The runners and the community

Ocarina of Time's competitive scene has been defined for years by ZFG, also known
as Zelda Freak Gohma, who has held and traded the top any% and 100% times and is
one of the most recognizable faces in the category. Earlier in the game's
speedrunning history, Cosmo was a prominent figure and helped popularize some of
the foundational glitches. The community runs deep: there is a thriving Ocarina of
Time bingo scene, an active randomizer where item locations are shuffled into a new
logic puzzle every seed, and a long-running argument about whether the game is now
effectively "solved."

## Beyond Ocarina

Majora's Mask is the sister game and its own discipline, built around the three-day
clock, the Song of Soaring for fast travel, and inventory tricks of its own. The
Wind Waker has famous large skips, including a barrier skip and a story skip that
cut huge sections out of the intended route. Across all of them the pattern is the
same: a beloved childhood game, taken apart by people who love it enough to break
it on purpose.
